Solutions

Taking on Challenges Facing The Entire Agribusiness Supply Chain

Problems

GrainChain’s suite of products solve some of the most pressing challenges for farmers, storage operators, buyers and the rest of the agribusiness supply chain:

Limited Transparency

Critical commodity provenance and ownership data does not zmove easily or reliably from one participant to another in the supply chain.

Inefficient inventory management systems

Too much of the agricultural supply chain relies on paper and manual data entry, creating long delays in the flow of information and increasing the opportunities for errors and fraud. Too often, Producers must wait weeks for confirmation on the quantity and squality of the product they have delivered to storage facilities.

20TH CENTURY LOGISTICS

Getting agricultural products to market is largely dependent on systems and technology from the last century. Reliance on scheduling, coordinating, and tracking deliveries via telephone and fax is outdated and unreliable.

LACK OF TRUST

Producers traditionally have not had the ability to conduct business reliably outside their immediate location or with partners other than trusted buyers from the past.

DELAYED PAYMENTS

Unpaid grain contracts, bankruptcy, or slow paying buyers cost the industry up to $40 billion per year.

DEPENDENDENCE ON LENDERS

Delays in payments at each stage of the supply chain puts Producers and others in agribusiness at risk, forcing them to rely on lending and struggling to avoid a cycle of debt.

INTERNATIONAL TRANSACTION RISKS

Currency fluctuations, extensive, burdensome and ever-changing export and import documentation requirements, logistics delays, and disparate legal systems all pose potential risks when dealing in international markets.
